Dearest Sarah,

How have you been since the last time we spoke?

A Courteous Check-In

I hope this message finds you enveloped in warmth and peace. It's been a little while since we last talked, and I often find myself wondering how you are doing.

I hope your days have been gentle and that you are smiling brightly — just as beautifully as I remember. I carry our memories lightly, with a quiet smile of my own.

There is something about your presence that leaves a mark — not just on moments, but on a person's way of seeing the world. I hope you know that.

Shared Energy & Understanding

I want you to understand that the fact I also went silent doesn't mean I gave up on you or stopped caring. I am also a plant that needs to be watered, just as much as I water others.

You know how it feels to pour energy out and not receive the same in return. I am human too, and being treated coldly hurts. It brings me peace when you consider my feelings alongside yours.

Writing this to you comes from a place of great honor and from the depth of the friendship we had. If I were viewing this merely from the angle of lovers, the anger of being hurt would have clouded my words.

We were smiling together, and then suddenly, you gave up. You left without even closing the door, as if the house that contained you was no longer of importance. I didn't deserve that. But in all that, my maturity and the bond we shared dissolves those feelings, and that's why I am able to keep pushing forward in a healthy way.

Respecting Your Journey

You told me you wanted to take a step back to find yourself, discover your own peace, and grow in the spirit. I want you to know how deeply I respect and admire that decision.

I don't want you to ever feel guilty for choosing yourself and your spiritual path. Growth is irresistible and absolutely necessary. You owe it to yourself to bloom fully.

I want you to know that choosing you, over and over again, isn't born out of desperation or weakness. It's the hallmark of a true man who stands by his words, his feelings, and his decisions. I care for you deeply, and I respect your space to flourish.
